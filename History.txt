version 01.01	12 minutes dev	2 minutes code gen	1 minutes test		15 minutes total

This is the start of a new game. This one will be implemented via Claude. This 
game I'm  trying for something that has the feel of an Atari (800 and/or 2600)
in the early 80's. The goal is not for a perfect Atari rendition, but to get 
some of its feel involved.
I received some ideas of games from various AIs (OpenAI, Claude, Gemini). Claude
has my favorite... kinda. It was a game called Crossroad. The playing field was 
a road intersection and the purpose... it wasn't clear, or if it was clear, it 
was easy to cheat. But it gave me the idea for this one. Consider this similar 
to Frogger, or even more specific, Freeway. You will drive a motorcycle back and 
forth, across two four-lane highways. Simple, fun, and exciting. To start, to 
summarize:
* There are nine rows of car lane. 
* There are the four lanes at the top. 
* There is a yellow center divider in the middle. 
* There are four lanes at the bottom.
* The player makes as many laps as possible, crossing the roads each time.
This game is playable with this little guidance. It's too difficult and there
is plenty to change. But I am amazed at the quality for just 15 minutes.
More details and refinement to follow.


version 01.02	21 minutes dev	6 minutes code gen	2 minutes test		44 minutes total

Changed the name from Crossroad to Crossroads. After all, there will multiple
roads. Think of a loop of sorts: up one road, down the other.
Add the crossroads. Two small lanes for the player's motorcycle, from top to
bottom.
The original version of the game allowed lateral movement. That's not what's 
intended. Limited to only up and down cursor keys.
Added some significant changes to add more details:
* The top 4 layers are a mirror image of the bottom 4.
* There will be 9 levels to the game. Each level last 20 seconds, nothing to
accomplish, just pure time for each level.
* The game is over after the 9 levels have passed (aka 3 minutes)
* The speed of the cars will be configurable. It will be by lane, by level.
This is the first glimpse of the game. Some changes remain, but primarily
focused on details, not featured.


Version 01.03	12 minutes dev	4 minutes code gen	2 minutes test		1 hour 2

To start, a bit of fine tuning. The speed of the motorcycle is set to 4 pixels
per second. The next thing was the main change for this version: implementing the 
loop. The player will start at the bottom of the left crossroad. When they get to
the top, the player is automatically moved off the top of the screen and into the
right lane. The player now travels down. When it to the bottom, the player is
automatically moved off the bottom of the screen and back into the left lane.
Finally was switch from 1 player life to unlimited life. If the player gets hit 
by a car, the game pauses for 500ms, then moves the motorcycle to the start of 
the current lane.


Version 01.04	6 minutes dev	2 minutes code gen	3 minutes test		1 hour 13

For previous changes, the entire app was recreated from scratch by Claude. The
app is built enough, we'll now focus on incremental changes.
This change adds scoring to the game. When a lap is completed, the player gets
points that match the level. For example, if they get to the other side of the
road and it's level 5, they get 5 more points. The score is displayed in the
upper-right corner of the game.


Version 01.05	8 minutes dev	1 minute code gen	3 minutes test		1 hour 25

The motorcycle seemed a little small. The motorcycle could wait between lanes of
cars. I asked Claude to make the car 20% larger. It is still (theoretically)
possible to fit between cars... but it has to be pixel perfect placement. Good
enough. Also, the motorcycle always faced in the same direction. I had Claude

Version 01.06	10 minutes dev	2 minute code gen	2 minutes test		1 hour 39

The UI for the motorcycle... wasn't quite right. It didn't look horrible, but 
wasn't easily recognizable as a motorcycle. I made a few changes and discovered
that the wheels were on sideways. Seems like a typical AI quirk. I had them 
turned the right way - it immediately looked a lot better.
The crash check was a little too unforgiving. The motorcycle and a car could 
collide and it would be fine. There was a buffer of 4 pixels. Switched it to
2 pixels to make it a little less forgiving.

Version 01.08	16 minutes dev	0 minute code gen	2 minutes test		2 hour 8

There is the level in the upper-left and the score in the upper-right. They

Version 01.09	18 minutes dev	2 minute code gen	6 minutes test		2 hour 34

I wanted to add some beeping and blinking at the end of each level. Give the 
player some warning that each level is about to end. I asked Claude to blink
the level once per second for the past 5 seconds, with a beep tone to go with
it. The blinking worked, but the audio didn't always happen. I let Claude know
and have it make some changes. It did, but it still made some changes. I 
noticed that the pause for the Level to be hidden (250 ms) did not match the
frame intervals (30 frames per second). This caused some irregularities. I 
switched the activities to match frames. That worked, with one exceptions.
There was a bug in the code: check that it was not Level 9. I allowed that.
After that, the beeping and blinking worked as intended.


Version 01.10	19 minutes dev	1 minute code gen	4 minutes test		2 hour 58

One of last majors featuring I wanted to add was sounds of cars as they passed
by the player. Volume of the sounds would be based on the speed of the vehicle 
and the distance away. Claude had many questions:
Q: Should the sound start when the vehicle enter the lane or only when it is a
	certain distance from the player.
A: When the car enters the player's center lane.
Q: What are the two roads?
A: The two that the player rides on.
Q: Do you want the sound in stero or is mono fine?
A: Mono is preferred.
Q: Is the sound dynamically based on distance from the player as the car moves?
	Or is it just a single spot that determines it?
A: Each car sound is based on when the car crosses into the zone.
Q: How is the sound based on distance?
A: 0 pixels away is the max. It descends volume with distance.
Q: Does the pitch vary by speed?
A: no
Like I said, a lot of changes. Many questions were asked as "one more question."
The initial version worked well. The car sound... well it did not sounds like a 
car. I asked Chat to try 4 different sounds for me and I will pick. It did. Lucky 
for me, I only liked one of them, but one is all I needed. I like the game with
this.
Time to get ready for the initial release!
